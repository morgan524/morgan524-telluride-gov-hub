Empowering Telluride: The Future of Lot L Development

Empowering Telluride: The Future of Lot L Development Staff Jul 27, 2025 2 min read Updated: Feb 18

The Importance of Community Input

The Town Council is currently studying the feasibility of developing a parking structure, affordable housing, and ancillary uses such as transit-oriented commercial uses on Lot L of the Backman Village Subdivision (Lot L). The massing concept plan for the parking garage

There is no doubt that on busy weekends, the need for parking in Town is at a very high demand. However, a parking lot of this size will dramatically change the nature and feel of Town forever. The convenience of allowing so much parking may well be offset by the increase in traffic and the substantial change of having a building this large on the edge of Town.

The Impact on Our Community

We are not saying whether this is a good or a bad idea, but surely the development of this magnitude deserves voters having the right to say yes or no. The decisions made about Lot L will affect all of us. It's essential that we, as a community, have a say in what happens in our own backyard.

Why Community Decisions Matter

When it comes to significant development projects, the voices of residents should be prioritized. After all, we are the ones who will live with the consequences. A project like this could reshape our Town for generations. Imagine walking through Telluride in the future. Will it still feel like home? Or will it become a bustling hub that loses its charm? These are the questions we need to consider.

The Role of Let People Decide

Let People Decide aims to empower the residents of Telluride, Colorado, by ensuring they have a direct vote on significant development projects and large public spending, rather than leaving these decisions solely to the Town Council. This initiative is crucial for maintaining the character of our community.

The Need for Affordable Housing

In addition to parking, the proposal includes affordable housing. This is a pressing issue for many residents. We all know someone who struggles to find a place to live in Telluride. Affordable housing can help keep our community diverse and vibrant.

Balancing Development and Community Needs

While development is necessary, it must be balanced with the needs of the community. We must ask ourselves: How can we ensure that new projects benefit everyone? The answer lies in open dialogue and community involvement.

Conclusion: Your Voice Matters

As the Town Council moves forward with its studies, let's remember that our voices matter. We deserve the right to weigh in on developments that will shape our Town's future. Let's advocate for a system where every resident has a say. Together, we can ensure that Telluride remains a place we all love and cherish.

In conclusion, the future of Lot L is not just about parking or housing; it's about our community's identity. Let's make sure we have a say in what happens next.
