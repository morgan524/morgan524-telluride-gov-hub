Empowering Telluride: The Future of Lot L Development

The Importance of Community Input

The Town Council is currently studying the feasibility of developing a parking structure, affordable housing, and ancillary uses such as transit-oriented commercial uses on Lot L of the Backman Village Subdivision.

There is no doubt that on busy weekends, the need for parking in Town is at very high demand. However, a parking lot of this size will dramatically change the nature and feel of Town forever. The convenience of allowing so much parking may well be offset by the increase in traffic and the substantial change of having a building this large on the edge of Town.

The Impact on Our Community

We are not saying whether this is a good or a bad idea, but surely the development of this magnitude deserves voters having the right to say yes or no. The decisions made about Lot L will affect all of us.

The Need for Affordable Housing

In addition to parking, the proposal includes affordable housing. This is a pressing issue for many residents. We all know someone who struggles to find a place to live in Telluride. Affordable housing can help keep our community diverse and vibrant.

Balancing Development and Community Needs

While development is necessary, it must be balanced with the needs of the community. How can we ensure that new projects benefit everyone? The answer lies in open dialogue and community involvement.

The future of Lot L is not just about parking or housing; it’s about our community’s identity. Let’s make sure we have a say in what happens next.
