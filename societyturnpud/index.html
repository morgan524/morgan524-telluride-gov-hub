As the Society Turns (the Survey Episode)

While our survey was not conducted with scientific rigor, the results are pretty much aligned with our expectations. We received 106 total responses, but 11 people failed to provide a name, so they were not counted.

Of those respondents, the vast majority (83%) were aware of a plan for a new hospital at Society Turn. However, the majority flipped on knowing the details of the overall PUD plans. 79% did not know the new hospital accounts for only about 10% of the total development. 79% did not know that the total PUD development is around 400,000 sf, mostly free-market commercial. 71% did not know the PUD included a 125-room hotel.

We did not ask people whether they support or oppose this PUD, as we were more interested in their basic knowledge of the development.

Results:

Question 1: "Did you know the Hospital District has been planning to build a new hospital at Society Turn (next to Lawson Hill) for the past few years?" YES (83%) NO (17%)

Question 2: "Were you aware that the new hospital will comprise approximately ten percent of a larger free-market commercial development?" YES (21%) NO (79%)

Question 3: "Were you aware that the overall PUD Development is approximately 400,000 square feet of mostly commercial development?" YES (21%) NO (79%)

Question 4: "Were you aware that the development includes a 125 Room hotel?" YES (29%) NO (71%)

From our unscientific survey, we see a serious disconnect between major project plans and the general population's knowledge of them. While we did not ask people about the town projects that are quite far along in the approval process, we assume the knowledge would be similar.

But when you add the development at Shandoka Lot L with 50 new residential units and 900 spaces of parking, 250 new residences and a hotel at Chair 7/Carhenge, and a redesigned gondola building and plaza, you have a substantial amount of new development. By our rough calculations, the total new square footage in town is about 775,000 sf, of which about 90,000 sf is new commercial space.

When added to the Society Turn PUD, as shown below, the total new construction exceeds 1,367,000 square feet of development in our small region, with close to 400,000 square feet of new commercial space that all requires employees to operate and staff.

This level of development is equivalent to 32 football fields of floor space or 8 Walmart Superstores. Can those who oppose Measure 300 understand why there is concern about this scale of development, which is currently under review for planning and approval?

Is this a binary issue? Either we accept all this development as approved by our elected representatives, or none of it happens.
