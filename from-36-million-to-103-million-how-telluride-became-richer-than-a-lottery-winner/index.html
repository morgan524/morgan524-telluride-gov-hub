From $36 Million to $103 Million: How Telluride Became Richer Than a Lottery Winner

The Town’s budget was a humble $10 million back in 2015. By 2025, this figure has surged to $103.3 million, representing a staggering 930% increase in just ten years.

How did a small mountain town experience such explosive budgetary expansion? This breakdown of where all that money went (and keeps going) is essential reading for any Telluride taxpayer who wants to understand the mechanics of municipal spending in a resort community.

The piece examines the various revenue streams, capital projects, and policy decisions that transformed Telluride’s finances from a modest mountain town budget into something that rivals much larger municipalities—and asks whether that trajectory is sustainable.
