Saturday Shot of Finance: If VooDoo Were a Private Development, Would It Already Be Bankrupt?

VooDoo Economics, The Telluride Edition

A local family of four recently shared on Facebook: “My family has lived here for ten years, so we are all too aware of the housing crisis... We are stuck in ‘affordable housing,’ and rent prices are soaring to unreasonable rates.”

This sentiment resonates deeply throughout the community. When subsidized housing fails to remain truly affordable or financially sustainable, it raises questions about whether consultants provided adequate financial guidance to town leadership.

The article explores the paradox of Telluride’s VooDoo affordable housing development—examining whether it could survive as a private enterprise or if it relies unsustainably on public subsidy.

With a $27.4 million price tag for 27 units, the per-unit cost of over $1 million raises serious questions about the definition of “affordable” housing when financing costs are included.
