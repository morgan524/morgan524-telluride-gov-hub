The Chair 7 Development Controversy

A hotel and commercial development on open space near the ski area is the most contentious proposal in years—here’s what the PUD amendment actually allows.

The Chair 7 development proposal would place significant commercial development, including a hotel, on land near the ski area that many residents consider de facto open space. The project requires a Planned Unit Development (PUD) amendment, which would fundamentally change what is permitted on the site.

This article examines the details of the PUD amendment, what it would allow, and why the proposal has generated more opposition than perhaps any other development project in recent Telluride history. For residents who care about the balance between economic development and preserving Telluride’s natural character, this is essential reading.
